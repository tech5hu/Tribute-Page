Life Story...

1942 January 17

Born in Louisville, Kentucky with the birth name Cassius Clay

1954 October

His bicycle was stolen and he told the police officer, Joe Martin, that he wanted to "whoop" whoever did it. Martin trained young boxers and started to train Clay, who won his first fight and was declared the 'future world champ'

1956

Wins the novice Golden Gloves Championship as a light heavyweight

1959

Wins the AAU light heavyweight title at the Golden Gloves Tournament of Champions

1960 September/October

In September he wins the light heavyweight gold medal at the Rome Olympics, his first major success in athletics.
In October he wins his first professional bout with Tunney Hunsaker

Read more
1963 June 18

Wins his first foreign professional bout with Henry Cooper in London before 55,000 fans

Read more
1964 February 25/26

Knocked out Sonny Liston. This defeat allowed him to become the heavyweight champion.
He joined the Nation Of Islam shortly after

Read more
1964 March 6

At the age of 22, he changed his name from Cassius Clay to Muhammad Ali

Read more
1966

Ali is drafted and he files for conscientious objector status as a Muslim minister

1967 April/June

In April, the government denies his filing for conscientious objector status and he was drafted into the Army. He attends the induction ceremony, but he refused to step forward.
In June, Ali is found guilty of refusing induction into the Army. His passport is revoked, he is stripped of his heavyweight title and faces a 5 year prison sentence and a 3 year boxing ban

Read more
1970 October 26

Ali returns to fighting in Atlanta and wins over Jerry Quarry

1971 March/June

In March, in a famous match called "The Fight of the Century," Joe Frazier knocked Ali down, but Ali got up. However, after 15 brutal rounds, it was still ruled that Frazier won.
In June, the Supreme Court reverses its 1967 conviction and ruled that he was not guilty of evading the military, since he should not have been drafted in the first place due to his religious beliefs

1974 January/October

In January, he won over Joe Frazier.
In October, he defeated George Foreman and reclaimed the title of Heavyweight Champion of the World. This fight was a very famous event known as the "Rumble in the Jungle," since the fight was at a stadium in Zaire

1975/1976 September/October

In September, Ali beats Ken Norton in Yankee Stadium in New York.
In October, Ali beats Joe Frazier in Manila.

1978 February/September

In February, Ali lost the heavyweight title to Leon Spinks in a split decision.
In September, Ali wins the heavyweight title back from Leon Spinks after a battle at the Superdome in New Orleans.

1980 December

Ali came back and fought with Harry Holmes. Ali lost the fight.
Ali came back and fought with Trevor Berbick, once again, Ali lost the fight.
Aged 39, Ali announces his retirement from boxing

1984

Ali is diagnosed with Parkinson's disease

Read more
1996

Ali lit the Olympic flame

1997

He is named Sportsman of the Century by Sports Illustrated

2016

After leaving the public eye, Muhammad Ali died on June 3 as the result of septic shock due to unspecified natural causes. He was 74 years old.
He was buried in Louisville, Kentucky. He planned his own funeral which included eulogies from former President Bill Clinton, sportscaster Bryant Gumbel and comedian and close friend Billy Crystal
